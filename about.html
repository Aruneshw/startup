Our Story

From a student team to a community-driven tech freelance ecosystem.

How It Started

Zero Gravity began in April 2026 at Bannari Amman Institute of Technology (BIT Sathy), Tamil Nadu, when a small group of engineering students decided to learn faster by building together.

We quickly realized that many startups, small businesses, and individuals face the same problem: finding reliable, affordable technical support for solving real-world challenges — bugs, coding issues, and full project builds.

At the same time, skilled college students like us lacked access to real-world opportunities to apply our knowledge, build credibility, and earn income. Existing platforms either focused only on freelancing or only on discussion-based solutions — none combined real problem-solving, trust, and skill validation.

That's why we built Zero Gravity: a tech freelance ecosystem that connects clients with college students and developers to solve real-world technical problems through a competitive, community-driven model.

"We don't just solve problems — we build a community where solving problems is how you learn, earn, and prove your skills."

— Zero Gravity
